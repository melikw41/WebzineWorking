A collection of Yale Fooball Events

In no particular order, with no heavy significance

September 9th, 2025

Without warning, Junior defensive end Zairon Jackson-Bass breaks into an acapella rendition of “Love” by Keyshia Cole Accompanied by Junior offensive lineman Zaiver Avera and Sophomore Receiver Thomas Bacon Jr

While freshman running back Hugo Djeumeni and Senior defensive end Melik Williams later joined and helped throw down a beat to bring it all together.
Head coach Tony Reno’s wife. Mrs. Reno proclaimed that the team must start an oragnized singing group for the team, which was met with agreement by Jackson-Bass and Avera.

September 3rd, 2025

A new trend has emerged from the late night dinners. Some athletes after finishing a long practice have decided to start their decompression from within the facility. With the facility accommodating this with the 2 flat screen tv’s in the dining hall, and gaming consoles in the locker room, they have almost no reason to be in a rush to get home. This merry crew of Yale football players has started to aptly refer to themselves as the “Late Night Crew”. While there is no clear founder this groups behavior can easily be traced back to the actions of senior Inumidun Ayo-Durojaiye . The members of the group vary by the day but the actions never change. Always present for late afternoon banter and often sought by younger players as a source of wisdom in the awkward times of adjusting to the college life. The Late Night Crew will always be present to break bread or watch whatever football game is on the tv that evening.

September 7th, 2025

After a grueling scrimmage, the player get a chance to rest and sleep in on this Sunday, not having any required meetings until noon.but to the player's suprise, today’s post-scrimmage practice is only in helmets and spiderpads ! A change up from the usual, which entails the required dress is helmets and shoulder pads. “This program has gone soft” claimed one upperclassmen. “Hey i’m not complaining” said another. The bulldogs continue to ready themselves for this final grueling week, with a surprising but welcome increase of rest on their war-torn bodies.

September 11th, 2025

The week is coming to a close and the football team was met with a practice schedule that was longer that the previous day's schedule.
Shocked and appalled, beaten and bruised, some bulldogs were, unsure of what to make of this complete change up. “We literally had to have practice cut short yesterday because all of our catapult numbers had us in the red [zone for overwork on our bodies].” procalimed senior Melik Williams . “You know what we should do?” questioned senior Dylan Yang , “Crank our numbers early and see if they cut practice again”. “I’m gonna do wind sprints during the warmups” Sophomore Brady Wagner added. Will this plan work, if they even attempt to execute it? Probably not, but jokes help to lighten th mood knowing the strain they put on their bodies on a daily basis.

September 23rd, 2025

The players have offically gotten into the swing of the fall, and with that comes the yearly plague that moves throughout the team. A cacophony of coughs and sniffles are heard throughout the meeting room. Senior defensive lineman Ezekiel Larry has just about the worst cough you could imagine, hacking and spitting between reps of scout, as is everyone else on the defense. Coach Elijah Weeks has been urging guys to take the Emergen-C that is kept in the nutrition center but will a little extra vitamin C be enough to keep away this illness? only time will tell
